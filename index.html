DAV Thrift Store Program

The Lubbock area DAV Thrift Store is a project of the DAV Department of Texas and sponsored by local chapter 44. Your donations of usable clothing and household items help support the activities of the DAV in Lubbock and surrounding areas. Clothing and other needed items are provided by referral to veterans in need at no charge. Money generated by the stores help fund DAV projects in the state of Texas.

Volunteer

There’s no better way than offering your time and energy as a volunteer to show your appreciation to the veterans of our country. We would appreciate and value your time in helping to keep the store merchandise organized and restocked, sort clothing, and perform other work as needed. Contact the store number with any questions regarding volunteering and so that we can learn the hours you are available and what type of work you would like to contribute.

We also work with the "Court Referral Program" as an alternate sentencing option for the Superior, Municipal, Traffic and Juvenile Court systems here in Texas. Community service is considered restitution by an offender through helping his or her community. The service means actions, activity and engagement -- doing something that needs to be done and that helps the community or a cause. Contact the store and let us know how we can help each other.

Items We Can Use

  • Appliances
  • Electronics
  • Jewelry
  • Kitchenware
  • Pots, pans, dishes
  • Toys / Games
  • Curtains / Drapes
  • Shoes
  • Bicycles
  • Books
  • Bedding
  • Seasonal items
  • Flat screen TVs
  • Computers
  • Exercise equipment
  • Usable furniture
  • Housewares
  • Large or built-in appliances
  • And many other things for the home

Items We Cannot Accept

  • Batteries
  • Bunk beds that are incomplete or do not meet *CPSC* standards
  • Household cleaners
  • Pianos or organs
  • Console TV’s or stereos
  • Paints and solvents
  • Anything with freon
  • *CPSC* recalled items
  • Large metal or wood desk
  • Items banned from sales
  • Wet, moldy, or greasy clothing or items
  • Tires and wheels
  • Sleeper sofas
  • Water beds including parts and accessories
  • Electronics from businesses (large quantities)
  • carpets or pieces of carpet
  • Furniture needing repairs or in unusable condition

IRS Rules for Clothing and Household Items

To be deductible, clothing and household items donated to charity after Aug. 17, 2006 must be in good used condition or better. However, a taxpayer may claim a deduction of more than $500 for a single item, regardless of its condition if the taxpayer includes a qualified appraisal of the item with the return. Household items include furniture, furnishings, electronics, appliances, and linens. See https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/p526.pdf
